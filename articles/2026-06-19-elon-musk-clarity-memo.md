## 💼 LinkedIn Post

**The Email That Eliminated Passive Hesitation**

October 4, 2021. Elon Musk sent an internal email to all Tesla employees with the subject line: "Please Note." It contained one of the simplest yet most brutal management frameworks I've ever seen.

He gave three options for anyone receiving explicit direction from him:

1. **Disagree and explain.** "Email me back to explain why what I said was incorrect. Sometimes, I'm just plain wrong!"
2. **Request clarification.** "Request further clarification if what I said was ambiguous."
3. **Execute.** Just do it.

And the kicker? "If none of the above are done, that manager will be asked to resign immediately."

**Why this works:**
The biggest killer of execution inside large organizations isn't bad strategy. It's passive hesitation. People nod in meetings, receive direction, and then... wait. Wait for approval. Wait for confirmation. Wait for someone else to make the first move.

Musk's system **forces clarity** — there is no neutral response. You either push back (with reasoning), ask a question (to understand), or act (without delay).

Speed compounds more than anything. In a world where most organizations are paralyzed by consensus and committees, this kind of radical accountability cuts through the noise.

It's not for everyone. It's demanding. But for teams that need to move fast and build hard things — it's a blueprint worth studying.
