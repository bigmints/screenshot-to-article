## 💼 LinkedIn Post

**Frontier AI, On Your Terms: Google's Gemma 4 12B Changes the Local AI Game**

Google DeepMind just released something that genuinely shifts the landscape for developers building AI-powered applications.

**Gemma 4 12B** is a dense, multimodal model designed from the ground up for local execution. And I mean *actually* local — not "theoretically runs on consumer hardware" local.

Here's why this matters:

**1. Real local performance**
It runs smoothly on laptops with 16GB VRAM. No cloud dependency, no API costs, no data leaving your machine. For edge applications, offline testing, and privacy-sensitive workflows — this is huge.

**2. Encoder-free multimodal architecture**
Rather than routing vision and audio through separate encoders, Gemma 4 12B feeds multimodal data directly into the LLM backbone. This is a first for a mid-sized open model, and it means native audio and vision processing without the usual latency overhead.

**3. Apache 2.0 + day-one ecosystem support**
It ships under Apache 2.0 with immediate compatibility across llama.cpp, Ollama, MLX, LM Studio, vLLM, and SGLang. The weights are on Hugging Face and ready to use *today*.

**4. Part of a broader family**
The Gemma 4 lineup spans 12B, 26B, and 31B variants — this is the sweet spot for developers who need reasoning power approaching the larger MoE models but at less than half the memory footprint.

Whether you're building local voice assistants, on-device document processors, or the next generation of agentic tools — this is the model to experiment with this week.

The weights are live on Hugging Face and ai.google.dev. Go build something.
